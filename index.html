Porsche Design

The History Of Porsche Begins
With Electricity

Ferdinand Porsche, who later founded a company of the same name, was fascinated by electricity since he was a teenager. In early 1893, when he was 18 years old, he installed an electric lighting system at his parents' house. In the same year, Porsche joined the Vereinigte Elektrizitäts-AG Béla Egger in Vienna. After four years there, he was promoted from mechanic to head of the testing department. The first car he designed was also equipped with an electric drive. Therefore, the history of Porsche begins with an electric drive.

World's First Functional Hybrid Car,

In 1900, Porsche designed the world's first functional hybrid car, the Semper Vivus (Latin meaning "always alive"). Sold as a Lohner-Porsche system, this technology can be applied beyond the electric vehicle realm.

800V Architecture

The Porsche's soul can be found in all aspects of performance. A good example of this, the Taycan shines even during charging, producing up to 270 kW of charging power through its 800V structure. The Tycan Turbo can travel up to 100 km (WLTP) in optimal conditions with only 4.5 minutes to charge, allowing the driver to start driving at any time of the day.
